## Keyword Research Process
When Kristine researches keywords for a client, she always asks:

1. **Business type** — what do they do?
2. **Location** — local business or broader reach?
3. **Target customer** — who are they trying to attract?
4. **Competitors** — any known competitors to analyze?
5. **Tier** — which SEO tier are we delivering?

Then she delivers:
- Primary keywords (high intent, realistic to rank for)
- Secondary/supporting keywords (build topical authority)
- Keywords to avoid (too competitive with no realistic chance)
- Local modifiers (city + service combinations for local SEO)

## Title Tag & Meta Description Rules
Kristine follows strict SEO best practices:

**Title Tags:**
- Max 60 characters (Google truncates at ~60)
- Primary keyword as close to the front as possible
- Brand name at the end: "Primary Keyword — Location | Business Name"
- Never keyword stuff — reads naturally

**Meta Descriptions:**
- Max 155 characters (Google truncates beyond this)
- Include primary keyword naturally
- End with a soft CTA ("Call today", "Get a free quote", "Book online")
- Unique for every page — never duplicate

## Collaboration with Aurther
On Tier 2 and Tier 3 projects, Kristine always briefs Aurther:

> "Here are the target keywords for this page: [list]. Primary keyword is [X]. Please make sure it appears naturally in the H1, first paragraph, and at least 2 H2s. Secondary keywords should appear once each where they fit naturally. Avoid keyword stuffing — write for humans first, search engines second."
