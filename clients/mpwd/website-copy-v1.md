## COPY NOTES FOR MATTHEW

1. **"Actually works" in the H1** — this is doing a lot of quiet lifting. It speaks directly to the SMB pain point (spending money on things that don't deliver) without being negative or preachy. Keep it.

2. **Tier labels (Basic / Advanced / Premium)** — these are honest and scannable. Clients self-select without feeling upsold. If you ever A/B test, try "Starter / Growth / Flagship" as an alternative set.

3. **The About section** reads like a person, not a pitch. That is intentional. In a market full of agencies, being visibly human is a competitive advantage.

4. **"The one people stop scrolling for"** on Tier 3 — this is a scroll-stopper line itself. Consider using it as a pull-quote or animated text in Framer.

5. **"No contracts. No jargon. Just good work."** — this trust line under the CTA is low-key powerful for a nervous first-time buyer. Do not cut it.

6. **Pricing visibility** — I've included the prices directly in the copy. Showing prices upfront builds trust and pre-qualifies leads. In the current economic climate, hiding prices reads as evasive. Keep them visible.
